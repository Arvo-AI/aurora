# Weekly Posting Blueprint

## How the System Works

We have **2 employee accounts** (Account 1, Account 2) that each exist on all 3 platforms — LinkedIn, X, and Reddit. That's 6 account-platform combinations total.

Each account posts every 2 days. On each posting day, both accounts publish — one content piece each. A single content piece is reused across all 3 platforms (adapted for format), so one piece of writing turns into 3 posts. But two different accounts never post the same content on the same platform — audiences on one platform should never see the same take twice.

## Rationale

- **2 accounts per platform** doubles our surface area without doubling content creation effort, since each piece is cross-posted to all 3 platforms.
- **Cross-platform reuse** keeps the workload manageable — we create 2 pieces of content per posting day, but get 6 posts out of them.
- **No same-platform overlap** ensures followers on any one platform see distinct content from each account, avoiding the impression of a coordinated spam operation.
- **Every-2-day cadence** keeps us consistently visible without burning out on content creation.

## The Numbers

- **4 posting days per week**: Mon / Tue / Thu / Sat
- **2 content pieces per posting day**: 1 per account
- **8 unique content pieces per week** (4 per account)
- **24 total posts per week** (8 pieces x 3 platforms)

## Content Types

We rotate across 3 content pillars so feeds stay varied:

- **Industry Commentary** — Developer pain points, outage reactions, timely takes
- **Original Research** — Uptime data, reliability trends, data-driven insights
- **Product Demos** — Feature walkthroughs, short demos (use Trupeer for video)

## Rules (Quick Reference)

- Each account posts on every platform every 2 days
- Reuse the same content across LinkedIn, X, and Reddit (adapt format per platform)
- Never post the same content from two different accounts on the same platform
- 8 content pieces created per week total (4 per account)
- Saturday is heavier on commentary since outages/industry news accumulate over the week
